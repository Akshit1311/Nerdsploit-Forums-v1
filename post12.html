SECURE PASSWORD

In this digital era, the amount of time an average person is spending online is constantly increasing. Various tasks are carried out online daily and there is always something new, that drag us back to our computers or smartphones.

80% of data breaches happen due to weak, reused or stolen passwords. Thus it is important to create a strong and secure password. A strong password acts as a lock or a layer of protection for your personal and valuable information.

Mistakes you often make:

1. Nowadays, a person has accounts on various online platforms. But to make it easier to remember, they use 5-6 passwords for all the accounts. This way it becomes easier for the hackers to gain access over your other accounts by cracking just one password.

2. People often save their login credentials and passwords on their system, which is not 100% secure.

3. Never set a password with a mindset that it is not something important, but it can make you extremely vulnerable. For example, your email id password. Your email id might be connected with your bank account or social media and if a hacker cracks it, it can cause severe losses.

How to create a strong password:

1. Make your password long enough:

Try to keep your password at least 12-14 characters long. If the password will be short then it could be easily deciphered.

2. Use a mix:

While creating the password, include lower and upper case letters, numbers and different types of symbols so that it becomes harder to crack.

3. Avoid personal information:

Avoid keeping your personal information such as your name, date of birth or combination of both as your password. It will be easy to crack, especially if someone knows you well.

4. Don’t use common words or phrases:

Words like default, blank and password are often used as passwords. These words are pathetically weak and can be easily guessed by the hacker. Avoid using obvious dictionary word or a combination of dictionary words. The use of common phrases like a movie dialogue is also not recommended.

5. Don’t reuse your passwords:

Don’t use the same password for email or other websites as if any one of those experience a breach, you may risk other services of being breached as well.

6. Use Two-Factor Authentication:

With this, you receive text messages or emails for login and password reset. Also during net banking, One Time Password(OTP) is generated which is sent only on the registered phone number.

7. Change your password regularly.

8. Do not share your username or password with anyone and if you had shared, change it immediately.

9. Don’t save passwords or use “remember me” on public computers.

10. Use a password manager.
