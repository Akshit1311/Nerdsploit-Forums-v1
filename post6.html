BRUTE FORCE ATTACK

In the world of cyber threats, Brute force attack is one of the easiest ways to get access to any website or server. It tries numerous combinations of usernames and passwords until they find the right one. It is just like trying all the possible keys to open a lock.

This might seem easy to you but it’s not.

Generally, every account has a username (for eg. Admin) and a password. All you have to do is to guess the password. If it's a 2-digit password then it will be simple for you because the number of potential combinations would be 100. After all, the numbers are from 0 to 9.

But in reality, no password in this world consists of 2 characters. Even the smallest pin or password used in banks is of 4 characters. And, on online platforms, the minimum password length is 8 characters. To make the password complex and secure, a combination of upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols are used. This might result in the number of potential combinations reaching up to trillions and therefore being difficult to crack.

Some of the notable brute force attack cases:

1) In 2013, WordPress was the target of a brute force attack from 90,000 IP addresses. The hackers tried to reach admin accounts by using different weak passwords. It was one of the largest attacks but fortunately, very few sites have been hacked in the process.

2) In 2013, GitHub also became the victim of a brute force attack. The hackers used 40,000 unique IP addresses to execute the attack. The attack was targeted on the accounts having weak passwords.

3) In 2015, TaoBao an e-commerce platform owned by the AliBaba group suffered from a massive brute force attack. Around 21 million user accounts were compromised.

Hackers motive behind the attack:

Hackers want to obtain unauthorized access to a specific web site through this attack. They may use this access to execute some other type of attack, steal valuable information, or shut down the website. The attacker can also infect the website with malicious code for long term goals without leaving any trace. Therefore, frequent scanning of the website is suggested, so that any malicious activity can be identified as soon as possible.

Types of brute force attack:

1. Reverse brute force attack:

In this, the hackers use the same password but keep changing the username until they get the correct combination.

2. Dictionary attack:

Hackers use well-known phrases, commonly used words such as “admin”, “password” or use dictionary words as the password.

3. Credential stuffing:

Hackers use known credentials such as email addresses and passwords that they have gained from previous breaches to log into an account. How to protect yourself from such attacks?

1) Increase the length of the password as a longer password will take more time to brute force crack.

2) Try to keep the password complex by using different variables.

3) Don’t use the same password on other platforms. The hacker may reuse the same information to gain access to other services.

4) Captcha can be implemented to verify whether a human is visiting the website or it’s a brute force attack.

5) Login attempts should be limited to a particular number. If an attempt is made more than the specified limit that IP address should be blocked for some while to stop further attempts.

6) Two-factor authentication should be implemented, as human involvement is needed. It adds an extra layer of security and can stop a brute force attack.
