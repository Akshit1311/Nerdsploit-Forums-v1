CYBER ATTACKS

The internet has changed our lives and has been one of the most fast-growing and transformative technology. The internet has grown exponentially in popularity and usefulness in the last 24 years. People are using the internet for various purposes be it entertainment, communication, work, research or e- commerce. Up to 57% of the global population is of internet users. A person spends an average of 6 hours and 42 minutes each day online.

Cyberattacks have the ability to affect anything, because we spend too much time online.

What is a cyber attack?

Cyber attacks can be simply defined as the deliberate exploitation of computer systems or networks. It uses malicious codes to alter computer data or code, which can result in disruptive consequences that can range from security breaches to identity theft. Cyber attacks can also be associated with cyber terrorism and cyber warfare. Cyber attacks take place online.

How do cyber attacks happen?

Different technical methods are deployed by cybercriminals for gaining access to computer systems or networks. Some commonly used ones are as follows:

1. Malware

2. Phishing

3. Ransomware

4. Denial of service

5. Man in the middle

6. Cryptojacking

7. SQL injection

8. Zero-day exploits

How can cyber-attacks be prevented?

Many people become a victim of cyber attacks mainly because of poor security measures taken by them. As an end-user, you cannot fully rely on the company that is providing the service. Therefore, anyone using the internet should adopt some basic precautions.

1. IDENTIFY THE THREAT:

It is important to identify the threat before it causes any severe losses.

2. USE AN INTERNET SECURITY SUITE:

Use an antivirus program combined with an internet security program, this will help you in:

i. Avoiding malicious installs and downloads done by mistake.

ii. Protection from the damages caused by Trojan horses.

iii. Prevention from being a victim of Man in the Middle.

iv. Protection against phishing.

3. USE A STRONG PASSWORD:

Creating a strong password can also help protect against data breaching. Following points should be kept in mind while creating a password:

i. Create unique passwords that do not contain any personal information like your name or date of birth.

ii. Try to keep your passwords long, at least 10 characters or more and use upper and lower case letters, numbers and symbols.

iii. Never use sequential numbers, letters or the word password itself as your password.

iv. Don’t use the same password everywhere and keep changing it regularly.

v. While creating a password incorporates acronyms in a phrase.

4. KEEP YOUR SOFTWARE UP-TO-DATE:

This is important for your operating system and internet security program. Despite being secure software when it is used on a larger scale, companies become aware of the flaws in the software. That is why they release frequent updates to patch these vulnerabilities.

5. AVOID IDENTITY THEFT:

Identity theft can cause more serious damage than financial losses. Identity theft is when someone uses your personal information to gain benefits in your place. Following points should be kept in mind while dealing with personally identifiable information –

i. Do not post personal information on social media.

ii. Never share your OTP with someone else.

iii. Don’t fill personal data on any website that seems suspicious or claims to offer benefits.

6. TAKE APPROPRIATE ACTIONS IF YOU BECOME A VICTIM:

i. Isolate the affected systems and servers. These should be

disconnected from all the other devices to prevent the malware from spreading.

ii. File a formal complaint with the police or to the concerned authorities.

iii. Reset the password of the accounts and websites that were having the same password as the compromised one.
